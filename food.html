7 Nutritionist-Approved Tips to Fight the Holiday Slump

By Acelya Erdogan

3 comments

Haylie Pomroy, author of New York Times best-seller The Fast Metabolism Diet: Eat More Food and Lose More Weight as well as the upcoming The Burn: Why Your Scale Is Stuck and What to Eat About It knows a thing or two about nutrition. (Which is why everyone from Jennifer Lopez to Reese Witherspoon to Cher trusts Pomroy for her expertise.) Here, in the thick of the season of family feasts, office parties, and glorious cookie platters, she explains how to imbibe and indulge without slipping into an interminable food coma.

Get your metabolism humming. "Eat five times per day: Have three healthy meals and two protein-based snacks every day [leading up to the holidays]. This will keep your metabolism stable and blood sugar normal, so when the holiday festivities kick in, your metabolism is all fired up and ready to deal with whatever you throw at it."

Plan ahead of time—and chill. "Eating right way before you have to go to a holiday event (and feeling positive about going) will keep the stress hormones at bay—and your food cravings down."

You can get away with a drink or two... "...But go for clear liquors like gin, rum, and vodka. They are cleaner, with fewer added chemicals and fake ingredients. Just keep in mind that alcohol is very dehydrating, so always drink eight ounces of spring water for every drink."

And skip the beer altogether. "If you want to keep your metabolism humming, beer is not the best choice. It has more carbohydrates than wine or liquor."

Warm up with protein-heavy appetizers. "When you know you'll be eating a lot, aim to eat 10 to 15 grams of protein every two hours—think cold cuts, deviled eggs, shrimp cocktail, turkey jerky, or hummus and veggies. It'll tell your metabolism that it's time to really kick into high gear. Remember, your body can handle just about anything as long as your metabolism is ready for it."

Favor homemade sweets (which taste better anyway, in our opinion). "If you want dessert, try to avoid store-bought goodies. Aim to bake your own treats. Baking at home is healthier, made with love, and metabolizes faster [because these foods are less processed and don't usually include as many artificial ingredients]."

But all in all, eat, drink, and be merry. "Eating with enjoyment releases feel-good endorphins, which in turn prompts your thyroid to release fat-burning hormones—and lower another hormone, leptin. This healthy hormone chain of events will encourage your body to release stored fat for fuel helping you feel full and preventing you from overeating."
